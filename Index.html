Hotel Reception Conversation

Receptionist: Good evening! Welcome to Sunrise Grand Hotel. How may I assist you tonight?

Guest: Hi, I have a reservation under the name Emily Torres. I'm here for the Global Marketing Conference.

Receptionist: Let me check... Yes, I see your name. You've booked a deluxe single room with a balcony, for two nights. Is that correct?

Guest: Yes, that's right. I'll also need a quiet space to work in the evenings. Does the room have a desk?

Receptionist: Yes, your room includes a work desk and a comfortable chair. There's also complimentary high-speed Wi-Fi available throughout the hotel.

Guest: Great! I'll need to join some online meetings. Is breakfast included?

Receptionist: Absolutely. We serve a continental breakfast daily, from 6:30 to 10 a.m., in the restaurant downstairs.

Guest: Perfect. I also have a presentation tomorrow morning. Could I request a wake-up call at 6 a.m.?

Receptionist: Certainly, I'll set that up for you. Would you like a brochure with details on our services and local attractions?

Guest: Yes, please. I'd like to know about any quiet spots nearby to relax or take a walk after work.

Receptionist: You might enjoy the Riverside Park—it's a 10-minute walk from here. Also, our rooftop lounge offers a peaceful view in the evening. You can find both listed in the brochure.

Guest: That sounds perfect. One last thing—where's the elevator?

Receptionist: Just around the corner to your left. Your room is on the fifth floor, number 514. Here's your key card. If you need anything else, please dial zero from your room phone.

Guest: Thank you so much. I really appreciate the help.

Receptionist: You're welcome, Ms. Torres. Good luck with your presentation and enjoy your stay with us!

Pronunciation Practice: Rising & Falling Intonation

In English, the way your voice rises or falls can completely change how natural and clear you sound. This exercise will help you practice using intonation to speak more fluidly and confidently.

↘️ Part 1: Falling Intonation (Statement, Command, or WH-Question)

These examples come from real hotel conversations and reflect confident, complete thoughts.

  • Good evening! Welcome to Sunrise Grand Hotel. (Falling – Greeting)
  • I have a reservation under the name Emily Torres. (Falling – Statement)
  • You've booked a deluxe single room with a balcony. (Falling – Statement)
  • Your room is on the fifth floor, number 514. (Falling – Statement)
  • That sounds perfect. (Falling – Agreement)

↗️ Part 2: Rising Intonation (Yes/No Question or Uncertainty)

These questions reflect how hotel staff and guests interact in a conversation.

  • How may I assist you tonight? (Rising – Offer of help)
  • Is that correct? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • Does the room have a desk? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • Is breakfast included? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • Could I request a wake-up call at 6 a.m.? (Rising – Polite request)
Helpful Tip:

• ↘️ Falling Intonation = shows confidence, finality, or a complete idea.

• ↗️ Rising Intonation = shows a question, uncertainty, or a request for confirmation.

• Practicing intonation helps you sound more fluent, natural, and professional in any conversation.
